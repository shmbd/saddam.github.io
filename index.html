About Me

The most interesting subject area to me has been 𝐌𝐢𝐜𝐫𝐨𝐛𝐢𝐨𝐥𝐨𝐠𝐲, and more particularly, its application to the control of
infectious diseases and promotion of human health. My laboratory experiences in microbial genomics, antimicrobial discovery,
and probiotic research have further enhanced my desire to know microbial mechanisms with practical applications.
One of my major achievements was the complete genome sequencing of the Hepatitis B virus (HBV) from clinical isolates.
Through Sanger sequencing and the use of bioinformatics tools such as BLAST and SeqMan, I detected mutations in the HBV
that were associated with antiviral drug resistance. The project not only furthered my skills in molecular methods and
data analysis but also contributed to a broader study on regional strain diversity of HBV, which was subsequently
published in a peer-reviewed journal.
